## Capsule Wardrobe System

### Principle: Mix & Match
Pack pieces that ALL work together. Every top pairs with every bottom.
Aim for 3 bottoms × 4-5 tops = 12-15 unique outfits from 7-8 items.

### Color Strategy
1. Pick 1 neutral base (navy, black, khaki, grey)
2. Pick 1 accent color (works with base)
3. All pieces coordinate within this palette

### Trip Length Guide

| Trip Length | Bottoms | Tops | Layers | Shoes | Total Items |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Weekend (2-3 days) | 1-2 | 2-3 | 1 | 1-2 | 6-8 |
| 1 Week | 2-3 | 4-5 | 1-2 | 2 | 10-12 |
| 2 Weeks | 3-4 | 5-6 | 2 | 2-3 | 13-16 |
| 1 Month+ | 3-4 | 5-7 | 2-3 | 2-3 | 15-18 |

> **Rule:** More than 2 weeks? You're doing laundry — pack for 1 week.

### Laundry Planning
- **Hotels:** Laundry service (expensive — $5-15/item)
- **Airbnb:** Usually has washer
- **Laundromat:** Search "laundromat near [hotel]" — $5-10/load
- **Hand wash:** Pack travel detergent sheets, use sink
- **Dry time:** Quick-dry fabrics essential for hand washing

### Fabric Strategy
| Fabric | Good For | Avoid |
|---|---|---|
| Merino wool | Base layers, socks (odor-resistant, temp regulating) | None — best travel fabric |
| Nylon/polyester blend | Pants, shells (quick-dry, packable) | Heavy sun (can be hot) |
| Cotton | Casual wear (comfortable) | Anything requiring quick dry |
| Linen | Hot climates (breathable) | Wrinkles badly in suitcase |
| Down | Jackets (warmth-to-weight ratio) | Gets useless when wet |
| Gore-Tex | Rain gear (waterproof breathable) | Expensive but worth it |

### Packing Method
1. **Roll** everything (saves 30% space vs folding)
2. **Packing cubes:** 1 per category (tops, bottoms, underwear, misc)
3. **Compression bags:** For bulky items (jacket, sweaters)
4. **Shoes:** Stuff socks inside, pack in shoe bags
5. **Heaviest items:** Wear on travel day (boots, jacket)
