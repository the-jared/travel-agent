# Travel Insurance Claim Playbook

## Claim-Ready Documentation (Gather DURING the Incident)

### Flight Delay/Cancellation
- [ ] Screenshot of flight status showing delay/cancellation
- [ ] Screenshot of airline notification (email, app, SMS)
- [ ] Boarding pass (original)
- [ ] Receipts for meals, accommodation during delay
- [ ] Receipt for alternative transport if rebooked independently
- [ ] Airline's written confirmation of delay and reason
- [ ] EU261 claim form (if applicable — EU flights)

### Lost/Delayed Luggage
- [ ] Property Irregularity Report (PIR) from airline — get at airport
- [ ] List of items in lost luggage with estimated values
- [ ] Receipts for emergency replacement purchases
- [ ] Original receipts for high-value items (if available)
- [ ] Follow-up reference number from airline
- [ ] Photos of luggage and contents (if you have pre-trip photos)

### Medical Emergency
- [ ] Doctor/hospital report with diagnosis
- [ ] All medical receipts (consultation, medication, tests, procedures)
- [ ] Pharmacy receipts
- [ ] Ambulance receipt
- [ ] Prescription copies
- [ ] Proof that condition wasn't pre-existing (if asked)
- [ ] Record of communication with insurer during treatment

### Theft
- [ ] Police report — file within 24 hours (CRITICAL for claims)
- [ ] List of stolen items with values
- [ ] Photos of stolen items (if available)
- [ ] Proof of ownership (receipts, credit card statements, photos)
- [ ] Witness statements (if available)
- [ ] Hotel incident report (if stolen from room)

### Trip Cancellation/Interruption
- [ ] Proof of reason (medical certificate, death certificate, employer letter)
- [ ] Booking confirmations for cancelled arrangements
- [ ] Cancellation confirmations showing refund amounts
- [ ] Receipts for non-refundable expenses
- [ ] Communication with travel providers about cancellation

## Filing the Claim

### Step-by-Step Process
1. **Notify insurer immediately** — Most require notification within 24-48h
   - Phone: [Hotline number from policy]
   - Online: [Claims portal URL]
   - Reference: [Policy number]

2. **Gather documentation** (see checklists above)

3. **Complete claim form** — Be specific, factual, detailed
   - Include timeline of events
   - List all expenses with receipts
   - Explain why each expense was necessary

4. **Submit everything at once** — Don't send partial claims
   - Keep copies of everything you submit
   - Send via tracked method / screenshot submission confirmation
   - Note date submitted and claim reference number

5. **Follow up**
   - If no response within 14 days, call
   - Document every communication (date, name, what was said)
   - Ask for expected timeline

6. **If denied**
   - Request written explanation
   - Review policy language carefully
   - Appeal with additional evidence
   - Contact insurance ombudsman if appeal fails
   - Credit card company may also cover (if paid with card)

## What's Usually Covered vs. Not

### Typically Covered
- Medical emergencies abroad
- Emergency evacuation / repatriation
- Trip cancellation for covered reasons (illness, death, natural disaster)
- Flight delays over 6-12 hours
- Lost/stolen luggage and personal effects
- Emergency accommodation during delays

### Typically NOT Covered
- Pre-existing conditions (unless declared and covered)
- Adventure sports (unless add-on purchased)
- Alcohol/drug-related incidents
- Items left unattended
- War zones / destinations with travel advisories
- "Change of mind" cancellations
- Pandemic-related issues (varies by policy)
- Cash theft over small limits ($200-500 typical)
- Electronics over certain limits ($500-1000 typical)
- Single-item limits (check policy — often $250-500 per item)

## Pro Tips
- **Photo everything before the trip:** Suitcase contents, electronics serial numbers, valuables
- **Keep receipts digitally:** Snap photos of all receipts daily
- **Travel with 2 credit cards from different banks:** Backup if one is compromised
- **Credit card travel insurance:** Many premium cards include substantial coverage — check before buying separate insurance
- **Annual multi-trip policy:** Cheaper than per-trip if you travel 3+ times/year
