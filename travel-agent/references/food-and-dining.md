# Food & Dining Research Guide

## Research Protocol

### Sources (Search ALL)

1. **Eater** — City guides, heatmaps, "Where to Eat" roundups
2. **Michelin Guide** — Stars AND Bib Gourmand (affordable excellence)
3. **TripAdvisor** — Filter: top-rated, "local cuisine", "hidden gem" tag
4. **Google Maps** — Sort by rating, read recent local-name reviews
5. **Local review platforms:**
   - Japan: Tabelog (the gold standard — 3.5+ is excellent)
   - Korea: MangoPlate, Naver
   - Thailand: Wongnai
   - France: Le Fooding
   - Italy: Gambero Rosso, Dissapore
   - Spain: El Tenedor
6. **YouTube** — "[city] food tour", "[city] street food", "[city] must eat"
7. **Reddit** — r/FoodTravel, r/[destination]food
8. **Local food blogs** — Search in local language: "[city] food blog [year]"
9. **Instagram** — Location-tagged food posts for visual curation

### Dietary Research (Always Include)

For EVERY restaurant recommended, note:
- Vegetarian options available?
- Vegan options available?
- Gluten-free options available?
- Halal options available?
- Nut allergy safety?
- Language barrier for communicating allergies?

Include a "Dietary Survival Guide" section with:
- How to say "I am allergic to [X]" in local language
- How to say "No [meat/dairy/gluten]" in local language
- Allergy card recommendation (printable card in local script)
- Safe restaurant chains for specific dietary needs
- Grocery stores / health food shops for self-catering

### Research Categories

#### Must-Try Local Dishes
- The 10-15 dishes that define the cuisine
- Where to get the BEST version of each
- Price range for each
- Vegetarian/alternative versions where applicable

#### Restaurant Tiers

**Fine Dining (Splurge)**
- Michelin-starred or equivalent
- Reservation required? How far in advance?
- Dress code? Price per person?

**Mid-Range (Great Value)**
- Best reviewed in the $15-40/person range
- Local favorites, not tourist traps
- Reservation needed or walk-in?

**Street Food & Markets**
- Specific stalls/vendors by name
- Market hours and best time to go
- What to order at each stall
- Hygiene tips

**Budget (Under $10/person)**
- Chain restaurants with reliable quality
- University-area cheap eats
- Self-catering: best grocery stores and local markets
- Convenience store culture (Japan, Korea, Taiwan — konbini meals)

#### Food Experiences
- Cooking classes
- Food tours (walking, market, street food)
- Farm visits / vineyard tours
- Tea ceremony / coffee culture
- Night markets
- Food festivals during trip dates

#### Breakfast, Lunch, Dinner Structure
For the destination's eating culture, document:
- Typical meal times (late dinner in Spain = 9-10pm)
- Reservation culture (essential? walk-in? app?)
- Lunch set menus / prix fixe (often best value)
- Tipping at restaurants
- Water: free? charged? tap safe?
- Bread/cover charge customs (coperto in Italy)
