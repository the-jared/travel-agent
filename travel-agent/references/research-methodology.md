# Research Methodology — Multi-Source, Multi-Language

## Core Principle: Research Like a Local, Present Like a Concierge

Every destination search MUST use multiple source types AND multiple languages.
The goal is to find what a well-connected local would recommend, not what
the first page of English Google shows.

## Source Hierarchy (Search All, Cross-Reference)

### Tier 1: Authoritative Sources (Always Search)
- Government tourism boards (e.g., japan.travel, visitkorea.or.kr)
- Embassy/consulate travel advisories (travel.state.gov, smartraveller.gov.au)
- WHO health advisories for destination
- Visa requirement databases

### Tier 2: Expert Curated (Always Search)
- Lonely Planet destination guides
- Rough Guides
- Fodor's Travel
- Culture Trip
- Atlas Obscura (for unusual/hidden attractions)
- Eater (for food cities)
- The Points Guy / One Mile at a Time (for flights/hotels)

### Tier 3: Community & Reviews (Always Search)
- TripAdvisor (top-rated + "hidden gem" filters)
- Google Maps reviews (filter by recent, look for local reviewer names)
- Reddit (r/travel, r/solotravel, r/[destination], r/JapanTravel, etc.)
- Nomad List / Digital Nomad forums

### Tier 4: Booking Platforms (Always Search for Pricing)
- Booking.com (hotels, apartments)
- Airbnb (unique stays, local apartments)
- Hostelworld (budget travelers)
- Google Flights (flight comparison)
- Skyscanner (flight + car rental)
- Rome2Rio (multi-modal transport routing)
- GetYourGuide / Viator (tours and activities)
- Klook (Asia-focused activities and transport passes)

### Tier 5: Video & Visual (Search for Experiential Context)
- YouTube: "[destination] travel guide 2025/2026"
- YouTube: "[destination] hidden gems"
- YouTube: "[destination] food tour"
- YouTube: "[destination] things to avoid"
- Instagram location tags for visual vibe check

### Tier 6: Local-Language Sources (THE DIFFERENTIATOR)
- Search in the destination's primary language
- Use translated search queries (see Language Strategy below)
- Look for local blogs, food review sites, event calendars
- Cross-reference with English sources to identify what's being missed

### Execution Protocol

1. **Always search in English first** — establish baseline
2. **Then search in local language** — find what English misses
3. **Cross-reference** — if something appears in local sources but NOT English, it's likely a genuine hidden gem
4. **Translate and present** — always present findings in English with original-language name in parentheses
5. **Note source language** — tag findings with 🌐 (English source) or 🏠 (local source)

## Trend Analysis

### What's Hot vs. Hidden
- **Trending:** Search "[destination] 2026 travel trends"
- **New openings:** "[destination] new restaurant/hotel 2025 2026"
- **Seasonal:** "[destination] [month] events festivals"
- **Instagram-famous vs. actually good:** Cross-reference social media hype with review scores

### Freshness Filter
- Prioritize sources from the last 12 months
- Flag any recommendation older than 2 years with ⚠️
- Check if recommended places are still open (especially post-COVID)
- Verify operating hours and prices are current
