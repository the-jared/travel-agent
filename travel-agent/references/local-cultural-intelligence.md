# Local Cultural Intelligence Guide

## Core Principle

Cultural knowledge prevents awkward situations, shows respect, and unlocks
better experiences. This guide ensures every trip plan includes deep cultural
context — not just the obvious "take off shoes in temples" but the nuanced
social dynamics that make or break a travel experience.

---

## 2. Do's and Don'ts — Deep Cultural Guide

### Research Protocol
For each destination, search:
- "[country] cultural etiquette for tourists"
- "[country] things tourists do that locals hate"
- "[country] social customs" (in local language too)
- Reddit: "r/[country] tourist etiquette"
- YouTube: "[country] cultural mistakes tourists make"

### Exhaustive Do's & Don'ts Categories

#### Greetings & Social Interaction
- [ ] How to greet (bow depth, handshake firmness, kiss count)
- [ ] Eye contact norms (respectful or aggressive?)
- [ ] Personal space expectations
- [ ] Using first names vs. titles
- [ ] Gift-giving etiquette (when, what, how to present)
- [ ] Business card exchange customs (Japan, Korea)
- [ ] Conversation topics to avoid (politics, religion, salary)
- [ ] Compliment customs (accepting vs. deflecting)

#### Dining Etiquette
- [ ] Chopstick rules (Japan: never stick upright in rice)
- [ ] Left hand vs. right hand (Middle East, India, Africa)
- [ ] Finishing your plate (clean plate = still hungry in some cultures)
- [ ] Leaving food (Turkey: clean plate = host gave too little)
- [ ] Pouring drinks (Korea: pour for elders, never for yourself)
- [ ] Toasting customs (eye contact? who toasts first?)
- [ ] Slurping (Japan: polite. Italy: not polite.)
- [ ] Tipping (US: expected. Japan: insulting.)
- [ ] Splitting the bill (culturally normal or rude?)
- [ ] Waitstaff interaction (wave, call out, wait silently, press button?)

#### Public Behavior
- [ ] Queue culture (strict UK, flexible Italy, nonexistent in some places)
- [ ] Voice volume in public (Japan: whisper on trains)
- [ ] Phone calls on transit (taboo in Japan, normal elsewhere)
- [ ] Eating while walking (Japan: taboo. NYC: normal.)
- [ ] Chewing gum (Singapore: illegal to sell)
- [ ] Smoking rules and zones
- [ ] Jaywalking (Germany: social death. Vietnam: survival skill.)
- [ ] PDA (public displays of affection — varies enormously)
- [ ] Photographing strangers (always ask first)
- [ ] Drone laws (banned in many popular destinations)

#### Religious Sites
- [ ] Dress code (cover shoulders, knees, head?)
- [ ] Shoe removal (where to leave them, stolen shoe risk?)
- [ ] Photography allowed? (no flash? no selfie sticks?)
- [ ] Behavior (silence, no pointing feet at Buddha, etc.)
- [ ] Donation expectations
- [ ] Gender-segregated areas
- [ ] Entry requirements (non-believers allowed?)

#### Money & Transactions
- [ ] Bargaining expected, tolerated, or offensive?
- [ ] How to bargain (start at what fraction?)
- [ ] Tipping by service type (restaurant, hotel, taxi, tour guide)
- [ ] Rounding up on bills
- [ ] Paying for others (treat culture vs. splitting)
- [ ] Price for locals vs. tourists (dual pricing — accept or negotiate?)

#### Home & Personal
- [ ] Removing shoes at entrance (most of Asia, many European homes)
- [ ] Bringing gifts when visiting someone's home
- [ ] What gifts are taboo (clocks in China, knives in some cultures)
- [ ] Complimenting someone's possessions (Middle East: they may gift it to you)
- [ ] Asking to use the bathroom (normal vs. awkward)
