# Hidden Gems Methodology — Local Language Deep Research

## Philosophy

Tourist guides and English-language travel blogs surface the same 20 places
for every destination. The real magic of travel is finding what locals love —
the restaurant with no English menu, the viewpoint that isn't on Instagram,
the festival that only locals know about.

This methodology systematically uncovers these experiences by searching in
the destination's native language(s) and cross-referencing against English
sources to identify the gap.

## Step 2: Construct Local-Language Searches

For EACH destination city, run these search patterns in the local language:

### Food & Drink
- "[city] 地元の人が通う店" (places locals frequent)
- "[city] 穴場 レストラン" (hidden gem restaurants)
- "[city] 安くて美味しい" (cheap and delicious)
- "[city] 知る人ぞ知る" (known only to those who know)
- "[city] グルメ ブログ" (gourmet blog)

### Sightseeing & Experiences
- "[city] 穴場スポット" (hidden spot)
- "[city] 観光客が少ない" (few tourists)
- "[city] 地元おすすめ" (local recommendations)
- "[city] 散歩コース" (walking routes)
- "[city] 写真スポット 穴場" (hidden photo spots)

### Events & Seasonal
- "[city] [month] イベント" (events this month)
- "[city] 祭り [year]" (festivals this year)
- "[city] 期間限定" (limited time / seasonal)

### Nightlife & Entertainment
- "[city] 地元 バー" (local bars)
- "[city] ライブハウス" (live music venues)
- "[city] 夜景 穴場" (hidden night view spots)

> **Note:** The above examples are Japanese. Translate the INTENT of each
> search to the destination's language. The key phrases to translate are:
> - "hidden gem" / "secret spot" / "locals only"
> - "off the beaten path" / "few tourists"
> - "local recommendation" / "local favorite"
> - "cheap and good" / "best value"
> - "secret viewpoint" / "hidden photo spot"

## Step 3: Cross-Reference Protocol

1. **Gather** — Collect 20-30 findings from local-language searches
2. **Check against English** — Search each finding in English
3. **Score:**
   - 🏠🏠🏠 **True Hidden Gem** — Only in local sources, zero English coverage
   - 🏠🏠 **Under-the-Radar** — Minimal English coverage (1-2 blog posts)
   - 🏠 **Known but Underrated** — In English guides but not in top-10 lists
   - 🌐 **Already Popular** — Well-covered in English (skip, not a hidden gem)
4. **Verify** — Check Google Maps rating, recent photos, operating hours
5. **Present** — Only include items scored 🏠 or higher

## Step 4: Output Format

```
## Hidden Gems — [City]

### 🏠🏠🏠 True Hidden Gems (Not in Any English Guide)

#### [Name in English] ([Name in local script])
- **What:** [1 sentence description]
- **Why it's special:** [What makes this a gem]
- **Found via:** [Source — e.g., "Tabelog search for 渋谷 穴場 ラーメン"]
- **Location:** [Address + Google Maps link]
- **Hours:** [Operating hours]
- **Cost:** [Price range]
- **How to find it:** [Specific directions — "down the alley past the 7-Eleven"]
- **Pro tip:** [Ordering tip, best time to go, what to ask for]
- **Language needed:** [Can you get by in English? What to say in local language?]

### 🏠🏠 Under-the-Radar

[Same format]

### 🏠 Known but Underrated

[Same format]
```

## Step 5: Quality Filters

Before including any hidden gem, verify:
- [ ] Still open and operating (check Google Maps, recent reviews)
- [ ] Safe to visit (neighborhood, time of day)
- [ ] Accessible to tourists (even if not tourist-oriented)
- [ ] Worth the effort (travel time vs. experience payoff)
- [ ] Unique enough to justify inclusion (not just "another good restaurant")
