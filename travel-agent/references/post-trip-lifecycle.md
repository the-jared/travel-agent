# Post-Trip Lifecycle Guide

## The Trip Doesn't End at the Airport

A comprehensive travel agent handles the return too. This guide covers
everything from jet lag recovery to expense reconciliation.

---

## 1. Jet Lag Recovery Plan

### Westbound Travel (US → Europe/Africa)
- Jet lag direction: Body thinks it's earlier than it is
- Strategy: Stay up until local bedtime on arrival day
- Light exposure: Get bright light in the evening
- Recovery time: ~1 day per time zone crossed
- Melatonin: Take at local bedtime for 3-5 nights

### Eastbound Travel (US → Asia/Australia)
- Jet lag direction: Body thinks it's later than it is
- Strategy: Sleep on the plane if arriving in morning
- Light exposure: Get bright morning light
- Recovery time: ~1.5 days per time zone crossed
- Melatonin: Take at local bedtime, avoid morning light on arrival day

### General Recovery Protocol
1. **Day 1 home:** Light schedule, unpack, grocery shop
2. **Day 2-3:** Return to normal schedule, avoid napping after 2pm
3. **Day 3-5:** Full routine, exercise helps reset
4. **Hydration:** Extra water for 3 days post-flight
5. **Caffeine:** Only before noon for first week back

## 2. Customs & Duty

### Returning to the US
- **Duty-free allowance:** $800 per person
- **Alcohol:** 1 liter duty-free
- **Tobacco:** 200 cigarettes or 100 cigars
- **Prohibited items:** Most fruits, vegetables, meats, plants
- **Declare:** Cash over $10,000, commercial goods
- **Global Entry/TSA PreCheck:** Speeds re-entry significantly

### Common Customs Mistakes
- Forgetting about purchases made abroad (they count!)
- Not declaring food items (even packaged snacks)
- Bringing back prohibited souvenirs (ivory, certain woods, shells)
- Exceeding alcohol/tobacco limits

## 6. Photo & Memory Organization

### Recommended Workflow
1. **Day 1 home:** Import all photos to computer
2. **Back up:** Cloud sync + external drive
3. **Sort:** Create folders by day matching itinerary
4. **Cull:** Delete duplicates, blurry, bad photos
5. **Edit:** Basic edits on 20-30 hero shots
6. **Share:** Create shared album for travel companions
7. **Print:** Order prints of 5-10 favorites

### Digital Memento
Create a trip summary with best photos matching the itinerary —
a digital scrapbook version of the trip folder.

## 7. Travel Insurance Claims (If Needed)

### What to Document Immediately
- [ ] Photos of damage/incident
- [ ] Police report (theft — get report within 24h)
- [ ] Medical receipts and documents
- [ ] Flight delay/cancellation confirmation
- [ ] Receipts for emergency purchases (replacement items)
- [ ] Communication records (emails, texts)

### Claim Filing Steps
1. Contact insurer within the required timeframe (usually 30-90 days)
2. Complete claim form
3. Attach all documentation
4. Keep copies of everything submitted
5. Follow up if no response within 2 weeks
6. Appeal if denied (with additional evidence)
