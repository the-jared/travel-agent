# Safety & Health Research Guide

## Pre-Trip Health Checklist

### Vaccinations
- [ ] Check CDC destination page: `WebSearch "CDC travel health [country]"`
- [ ] Check WHO destination page
- [ ] Required vaccinations (entry requirement — e.g., Yellow Fever)
- [ ] Recommended vaccinations (Hepatitis A/B, Typhoid, etc.)
- [ ] Routine vaccinations up to date (MMR, Tdap, flu, COVID)
- [ ] Malaria prophylaxis needed? (prescription required — plan 4-6 weeks ahead)
- [ ] Japanese Encephalitis risk?
- [ ] Rabies pre-exposure recommended? (remote areas with dogs/bats)

### Medications
- [ ] Prescription medications — pack in original containers with letter from doctor
- [ ] Check if personal medications are legal in destination country
- [ ] Imodium / anti-diarrheal
- [ ] Oral rehydration salts
- [ ] Antihistamines
- [ ] Pain relievers (ibuprofen, acetaminophen)
- [ ] Motion sickness medication (if needed)
- [ ] Altitude sickness medication (if applicable)
- [ ] Insect repellent (DEET-based)
- [ ] Sunscreen (SPF 50+)
- [ ] Basic first aid kit

### Travel Insurance
**Always recommend travel insurance. Compare:**
- World Nomads (backpackers, adventure activities)
- Allianz Global Assistance (comprehensive)
- Safety Wing (digital nomads, monthly)
- Credit card travel insurance (check what's included)

**Ensure policy covers:**
- [ ] Medical evacuation
- [ ] Emergency medical expenses ($100k+ minimum)
- [ ] Trip cancellation/interruption
- [ ] Lost/stolen luggage and belongings
- [ ] Adventure activities if planned (diving, skiing, etc.)
- [ ] COVID-related cancellations
- [ ] Pre-existing conditions (if applicable)

## Safety Research

### Government Advisories
- [ ] US: travel.state.gov/destination
- [ ] UK: gov.uk/foreign-travel-advice
- [ ] Australia: smartraveller.gov.au
- [ ] Canada: travel.gc.ca

### Common Scams (Research Destination-Specific)
For each destination, search: "[city] tourist scams [year]"

Common categories to check:
- Taxi scams (rigged meters, long routes, "meter is broken")
- Restaurant scams (no prices on menu, surprise charges)
- Fake tickets/tours
- Gem/jewelry scams (common in Southeast Asia)
- Friendship bracelet / bird seed scams (common in Europe)
- Fake monks / donation scams
- Currency exchange scams
- ATM skimming locations
- Pickpocket hotspots
- Fake police
- Romance scams
- "Closed today" redirections

### Neighborhood Safety Assessment
For each area the traveler will visit:
- Daytime safety rating (1-5)
- Nighttime safety rating (1-5)
- Specific streets/blocks to avoid
- Well-lit main streets vs dark side streets
- Police/security presence level
