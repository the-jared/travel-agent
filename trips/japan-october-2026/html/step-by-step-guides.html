Step-by-Step Guides — Japan October 2026

Detailed navigation instructions between every major leg of the trip. Each guide includes platform numbers, ticket info, estimated times, and a Google Maps link.

1. Narita Airport → Shinjuku Hotel

Mode: Narita Express (N'EX) | Time: ~85 min | Cost: ¥3,250 (or JR Pass)

  1. Clear customs and follow signs to "Trains / 電車" on level B1
  2. Look for the JR East Travel Service Center (green signs) — buy a Narita Express ticket or activate your JR Pass here
  3. Tap through the JR ticket gate and follow signs to the N'EX platform (usually tracks 1-2 at Terminal 1, track 1 at Terminal 2/3)
  4. Board the Narita Express bound for Shinjuku (check the car — the train splits at Tokyo Station; front cars go to Shinjuku)
  5. Ride ~85 minutes; the train is reserved seating, so find your assigned seat
  6. Exit at Shinjuku Station — follow signs to the South Exit / 南口
  7. Cross the street to the Busta Shinjuku side; most Shinjuku hotels are within 10 min walk of the South Exit

Google Maps: Narita Airport → Shinjuku Station

Tips:

  • If arriving after 9 PM, N'EX service may be limited — check the last train (~9:45 PM from Narita)
  • Alternative: Airport Limousine Bus to Shinjuku (~100 min, ¥3,200) — easier with heavy luggage
  • Charge your Suica/Pasmo at the airport for local transit once you arrive

2. Shinjuku → Hakone (Romancecar)

Mode: Odakyu Romancecar | Time: ~85 min | Cost: ¥2,330 (base + surcharge)

  1. From your hotel, head to Shinjuku Station West Exit side
  2. Follow signs for Odakyu Line / 小田急線 (separate from JR gates — look for the blue Odakyu signs)
  3. Go to the Odakyu Sightseeing Service Center (ground floor near the West Exit) to buy a Hakone Free Pass (¥6,100 for 2 days) — this covers the Romancecar surcharge is separate
  4. Buy the Romancecar limited express surcharge (¥1,110) at the ticket window or machine — this is in addition to the Free Pass
  5. Go to the Odakyu platforms (usually platforms 2-3 for Romancecar) — check the departure board
  6. Board the Romancecar — all reserved seating. Window seats on the left side offer the best views
  7. Ride 85 min to Hakone-Yumoto Station / 箱根湯本
  8. At Hakone-Yumoto, exit and find the bus terminal directly outside for connections to your ryokan area

Google Maps: Shinjuku Station → Hakone-Yumoto Station

Tips:

  • Book Romancecar seats online at romancecar.com up to 1 month ahead — window seats sell out on weekends
  • The Hakone Free Pass covers buses, ropeways, boats, and the return trip (regular express, not Romancecar)
  • Front-observation car (展望車) is a special treat — book early

3. Hakone → Kyoto (via Odawara Shinkansen)

Mode: Local train + Shinkansen Hikari | Time: ~2.5 hrs total | Cost: Covered by JR Pass (or ~¥11,000)

  1. From your ryokan, take a bus or Hakone Tozan train to Hakone-Yumoto Station (Hakone Free Pass covers this)
  2. At Hakone-Yumoto, board the Odakyu Line to Odawara (3 stops, ~15 min) — use your Hakone Free Pass
  3. At Odawara Station / 小田原駅, exit Odakyu and follow signs to JR Shinkansen / 新幹線 (it's a short walk through the station)
  4. Tap through the JR Shinkansen gate with your JR Pass or buy a ticket at the machine
  5. Go to platforms 5-6 and board the Hikari or Kodama toward Kyoto/Shin-Osaka (do NOT take the Nozomi — it's not covered by JR Pass)
  6. Ride to Kyoto Station / 京都駅 (~2 hours from Odawara on Hikari)
  7. Exit the Shinkansen gate at Kyoto Station — you'll be on the Hachijo (south) side

Google Maps: Hakone-Yumoto → Kyoto Station

Tips:

  • Hikari stops at Odawara roughly every hour — check the timetable at JR East Timetable
  • Send luggage ahead to Kyoto via Takkyubin (luggage forwarding) from your ryokan for ¥2,000-2,500 — travel light on Shinkansen
  • Sit on the right side (seats D/E) for Mt. Fuji views on a clear day

4. Kyoto → Osaka (JR Special Rapid)

Mode: JR Special Rapid (新快速) | Time: ~29 min | Cost: ¥580 (or JR Pass)

  1. At Kyoto Station, enter the JR gates (central gate is easiest)
  2. Go to platforms 2-3 (JR Kyoto Line / Biwako Line)
  3. Board the Special Rapid (新快速 / Shin-Kaisoku) bound for Osaka / Shin-Osaka / Himeji
  4. Ride 29 minutes to Osaka Station / 大阪駅 (do not get off at Shin-Osaka unless your hotel is there)
  5. Exit at Osaka Station — the Central Exit / 中央口 leads to the main concourse with access to all lines

Google Maps: Kyoto Station → Osaka Station

Tips:

  • Trains run every 15 min; no reservation needed — just show up and board
  • This is a local commuter train, so it can be crowded during rush hours (8-9 AM, 5-7 PM)
  • If heading to Namba/Dotonbori area, transfer at Osaka Station to the Midosuji Line (red line, subway)

5. Osaka → Hiroshima (Shinkansen Sakura)

Mode: Sanyo Shinkansen Sakura | Time: ~1 hr 25 min | Cost: Covered by JR Pass (or ~¥10,000)

  1. From Osaka Station, take the JR Kyoto Line one stop to Shin-Osaka Station / 新大阪駅 (~4 min)
  2. At Shin-Osaka, follow signs for Shinkansen / 新幹線 and go through the Shinkansen ticket gates
  3. Go to platforms 20-27 (Sanyo Shinkansen)
  4. Board the Sakura or Hikari bound for Hiroshima / Hakata (avoid Nozomi — not covered by JR Pass)
  5. Ride ~1 hr 25 min to Hiroshima Station / 広島駅
  6. Exit the Shinkansen gate — the South Exit / 南口 leads to the streetcar (tram) and bus stops

Google Maps: Shin-Osaka Station → Hiroshima Station

Tips:

  • Sakura has comfortable 2+2 seating in regular cars (wider than Hikari's 3+2)
  • Reserve a seat in the non-reserved cars 1-5 if you don't want to queue at the ticket office
  • Ekiben (station bento) from Shin-Osaka are excellent — grab one for the ride

6. Hiroshima → Miyajima Island (Train + Ferry)

Mode: JR San'yo Line + JR Ferry | Time: ~55 min total | Cost: Covered by JR Pass (or ~¥590)

  1. At Hiroshima Station, enter the JR gates and go to platform 1
  2. Board the JR San'yo Line bound for Iwakuni (runs every 15-20 min)
  3. Ride ~26 min to Miyajimaguchi Station / 宮島口駅
  4. Exit the station and walk straight ahead ~5 min to the JR Miyajima Ferry Terminal (follow the crowds and signs)
  5. Board the JR Ferry (not the competing Matsudai ferry) — the JR ferry is covered by JR Pass
  6. Ride ~10 min to Miyajima Island — the JR ferry takes a route that passes close to the floating torii gate (especially the outbound afternoon trip)
  7. Exit the ferry terminal on Miyajima; the Itsukushima Shrine is a 10-minute walk to the right along the waterfront

Google Maps: Hiroshima Station → Itsukushima Shrine

Tips:

  • Last JR ferry back to mainland is around 10:15 PM — but last trains back to Hiroshima are earlier (~11 PM)
  • The torii gate is best seen at high tide for the floating effect or low tide to walk up to it — check tide tables
  • Deer roam freely on Miyajima — guard your food and paper maps

7. Hiroshima → Kyoto/Osaka (Return Shinkansen)

Mode: Sanyo Shinkansen Sakura/Hikari | Time: ~1 hr 25 min to Shin-Osaka, ~1 hr 45 min to Kyoto

  1. At Hiroshima Station, enter the Shinkansen gates
  2. Go to platforms 1-2 (Sanyo Shinkansen eastbound)
  3. Board the Sakura or Hikari bound for Shin-Osaka / Tokyo
  4. Exit at Shin-Osaka (transfer to JR for Osaka) or continue to Kyoto Station

Google Maps: Hiroshima Station → Kyoto Station

8. Key Station Navigation Tips

Shinjuku Station (World's Busiest)

  • 200+ exits — always know which exit you need before arriving
  • JR Lines: Central area (platforms 1-16)
  • Odakyu Line: West side, separate gate (for Hakone Romancecar)
  • Metro (Marunouchi Line): Underground, west side — follow red "M" signs
  • Key exits: South Exit (Busta Shinjuku bus terminal, hotels), East Exit (Kabukicho), West Exit (Odakyu, government buildings)
  • Tip: Use Google Maps indoor navigation — it works inside Shinjuku Station

Kyoto Station

  • Shinkansen gates: South side (Hachijo Exit) — platforms 11-14
  • JR local lines: North side — platforms 0-10
  • Bus terminal: North side, directly outside the Central Exit — this is where you catch buses to Kinkaku-ji, Fushimi Inari, etc.
  • Tourist Info Center: 2nd floor, north side — get a bus day pass here (¥700)
  • Tip: The station has a rooftop terrace (free) with great city views — take the escalators on the west side all the way up

Tokyo Station

  • Marunouchi side (west): Classic red-brick facade, closest to Imperial Palace
  • Yaesu side (east): Modern, shopping/restaurants, highway buses
  • Tokaido Shinkansen: Platforms 14-19 (central area)
  • Tohoku/Hokuriku Shinkansen: Platforms 20-23 (north area)
  • JR Yamanote/local lines: Platforms 1-10
  • Tip: Character Street (underground on Yaesu side) has official shops for anime/character goods

Osaka Station / Umeda Area

  • Osaka Station (JR): Above ground, main concourse
  • Umeda (Hankyu/Hanshin): Connected underground — follow signs carefully
  • Midosuji Line (subway): Underground, look for red "M" — this takes you to Namba/Shinsaibashi
  • Tip: The area around Osaka/Umeda has 3 different stations that are all walkable but confusingly named differently

Shin-Osaka Station

  • Shinkansen platforms: Upper level (platforms 20-27)
  • Local JR lines: Lower level — JR Kyoto Line to Osaka Station (platform 1-2, 4 min)
  • Midosuji Line (subway): Underground — connects to Namba without transferring
  • Tip: If you just need a quick Shinkansen transfer, stay on the upper level

General Navigation Tips

  1. Download Google Maps offline for all of Kansai and Kanto regions before you leave — station Wi-Fi is spotty
  2. Hyperdia or Navitime apps give exact platform numbers and transfer times for all Japanese trains
  3. Follow the Japanese commuters — during rush hour, stand in the marked queues on platforms
  4. Yellow tactile strips on platforms mark the safe standing line — never stand beyond them
  5. Ekiben shops are always near Shinkansen gates — grab a meal for long rides
  6. Coin lockers are at every major station (¥300-700 depending on size) — use them to ditch your bags for day trips
  7. IC cards (Suica/Pasmo) work on all local trains, buses, and convenience stores — charge at any station machine
  8. Last trains are typically around midnight — plan your nightlife accordingly
