## Airport Transfer Step-by-Step

### Narita Airport → Shinjuku Station (N'EX — Narita Express)

1. After clearing customs, follow signs to the **B1F JR ticket counter** in the airport basement
2. Purchase a **N'EX Tokyo Round Trip Ticket** (¥4,070 for foreign passport holders — show passport)
3. Alternatively, use a **Suica/PASMO IC card** (not valid for N'EX reserved seats, though)
4. Look for the **JR Narita Express** departure board — trains run every 30-60 minutes
5. Board the N'EX on the correct car (check your seat reservation car number)
6. Ride direct to **Shinjuku Station** — approximately **1 hour 20 minutes**
7. At Shinjuku, exit via the **East Exit** or **South Exit** depending on your hotel location
8. The train also stops at Tokyo Station (~53 min), Shibuya (~75 min), and Ikebukuro (~85 min)

**Quick reference:**
- First train from Narita: ~7:30 AM
- Last train from Narita: ~9:45 PM
- Cost: ¥4,070 (round trip tourist discount) or ¥3,250 (one-way regular)

[Narita Airport → Shinjuku on Google Maps](https://www.google.com/maps/dir/Narita+International+Airport,+Narita,+Chiba/Shinjuku+Station,+Tokyo/@35.7,140.0,10z/data=!3m1!4b1!4m14!4m13!1m5!1m1!1s0x6022f0a8b0e4b5e3:0x7a0babb4a3a9d0de!2m2!1d140.3929!2d35.7720!1m5!1m1!1s0x60188cd0d6b1ba1f:0x1c32a1f1ecacfdd5!2m2!1d139.7003!2d35.6896!3e3)

### Haneda Airport → Central Tokyo (Keikyu Line)

1. After clearing arrivals, follow signs to the **Keikyu Line** (京急線) in the basement level
2. Purchase a ticket from the machines (accept IC cards) or tap your **Suica/PASMO** directly
3. Board the **Keikyu Airport Express** or **Keikyu Limited Express**
4. Ride to **Shinagawa Station** — approximately **13 minutes** (¥300)
5. At Shinagawa, transfer to the **JR Yamanote Line** (the green circle line) for:
   - Tokyo Station — 7 min
   - Shibuya — 17 min
   - Shinjuku — 22 min
   - Ueno — 15 min
6. Alternatively, some Keikyu trains run directly through to **Asakusa** via Toei Asakusa Line (no transfer needed — check the train's destination board)

**Quick reference:**
- First train to Shinagawa: ~5:25 AM
- Last train to Shinagawa: ~12:05 AM (midnight)
- Cost: ¥300 to Shinagawa, ¥600-800 total to most central areas

[Haneda Airport → Tokyo Station on Google Maps](https://www.google.com/maps/dir/Haneda+Airport,+Ota+City,+Tokyo/Tokyo+Station,+Chiyoda+City,+Tokyo/@35.62,139.75,12z/data=!3m1!4b1!4m14!4m13!1m5!1m1!1s0x601860f07e24a0c3:0x2e1b7e016cb8e50!2m2!1d139.7798!2d35.5494!1m5!1m1!1s0x60188bfbd89f700b:0x277c49ba34ed38!2m2!1d139.7671!2d35.6812!3e3)

## Navigation Tips

### Google Maps Transit Is Exceptional in Japan
- Japan has the **best Google Maps transit coverage in the world**
- Real-time train delays, platform numbers, and exact fare breakdowns
- Always shows multiple route options with transfer details
- Works for JR, private railways, subway, bus, and even ferries

### Search by Station Name, Not Address
- Japanese addresses are notoriously confusing (block-based, not street-based)
- Always search for the **nearest station name** + landmark name
- Example: search "Senso-ji Asakusa" not "2-3-1 Asakusa, Taito-ku"
- Taxi drivers understand station names far better than street addresses

### Last Train Times — Do Not Miss These
| City | Typical Last Train | Notes |
|------|-------------------|-------|
| Tokyo | 11:45 PM - 12:15 AM | Yamanote Line last train ~12:15 AM |
| Kyoto | ~11:30 PM | Buses stop earlier (~9 PM) |
| Osaka | ~12:00 AM | Midosuji Line last train ~12:05 AM |
| Hiroshima | ~11:00 PM | Streetcars stop ~11:00 PM |

- **Missing the last train** means either a ¥5,000-10,000 taxi ride or waiting at a manga cafe / karaoke until 5 AM first trains
- Google Maps will **warn you** when the last train is approaching — pay attention to the alert
- Friday/Saturday nights are especially risky — trains are packed near last-train time

### IC Cards (Suica / PASMO)
- Buy a **Suica** or **PASMO** IC card at any JR station machine (¥500 deposit + charge)
- Tap in, tap out — works on virtually all trains, subways, and buses nationwide
- Also works at convenience stores (7-Eleven, Lawson, FamilyMart), vending machines, and coin lockers
- **Apple Wallet Suica** is the best option — add via Wallet app, charge with Apple Pay, no physical card needed
- IC cards work across all cities: buy once in Tokyo, use everywhere

### Luggage Forwarding (Takkyubin)
- **Do NOT drag large luggage on trains** — it blocks aisles, there is limited space, and Shinkansen overhead racks are small
- Use **Yamato Transport (Kuroneko)** or **Sagawa Express** to forward luggage between hotels
- Cost: ~¥2,000-3,000 per bag, next-day delivery
- Drop off at any convenience store (7-Eleven for Yamato, Lawson for Sagawa) or ask your hotel front desk
- Ship bags the morning you check out → they arrive at your next hotel by the following afternoon
- This is how Japanese travellers do it — embrace it
