# Activities Guide — Japan October 2026

> Curated experiences for a solo foodie traveler seeking cultural immersion.
> October is one of Japan's best months: mild weather (15-22C), autumn colors starting, fewer typhoons than September.

---

## Tokyo (4-5 Days)

### Temples & Shrines

**Senso-ji Temple (Asakusa)**
- Tokyo's oldest temple, founded 628 AD
- Arrive by 6:30 AM to avoid crowds — the Kaminarimon gate and Nakamise-dori shopping street are magical when empty
- Walk the full approach: Thunder Gate -> Nakamise-dori -> Hozomon Gate -> Main Hall
- Free entry to grounds; treasure house ~300 JPY
- Don't miss: Asakusa-jinja (adjacent Shinto shrine), five-story pagoda, incense cauldron ritual
- Nearby: Kappabashi "Kitchen Street" for knife shopping (10 min walk)
- Station: Asakusa (Ginza Line, Asakusa Line)

**Meiji Shrine (Meiji Jingu)**
- Dedicated to Emperor Meiji and Empress Shoken, set in 170 acres of forest
- Walk the gravel path through the torii gates — the towering camphor wood torii is Japan's largest
- Write a wish on an ema (wooden plaque, ~500 JPY)
- Check schedule for traditional wedding processions (weekends, usually 10 AM-2 PM)
- Free entry to main shrine; Inner Garden 500 JPY (iris garden, Kiyomasa's Well)
- Allow 1-1.5 hours
- Station: Harajuku (JR Yamanote Line) or Meiji-jingumae (Chiyoda/Fukutoshi Lines)

### Art & Digital

**TeamLab Borderless (Azabudai Hills)**
- Relocated to Azabudai Hills in 2024 — the new space is larger and more immersive
- Book tickets 2-3 weeks in advance online (advance purchase only)
- Tickets: ~3,800 JPY adults
- Wear white/light clothing for better interaction with projections
- Go on a weekday; arrive right at opening (10 AM) or after 5 PM
- Allow 2-3 hours minimum
- The EN Tea House inside serves tea with digital flowers blooming in your cup (~500 JPY)
- Station: Kamiyacho or Azabudai Hills direct access

### Neighborhoods & Street Culture

**Akihabara (Electric Town)**
- Multi-floor electronics stores: Yodobashi Camera (8 floors), Akky
- Retro gaming: Super Potato (5F, must-visit), Trader (multiple locations)
- Anime/manga: Mandarake Complex (8 floors of collectibles), Animate
- Maid cafes: @Home Cafe for the classic experience (~2,000 JPY with drink + photo)
- Gachapon: dedicated capsule toy floors in Don Quijote
- Best time: afternoon into evening when neon lights up
- Allow 3-4 hours to explore properly
- Station: Akihabara (JR Yamanote, Hibiya Line)

**Yanaka District**
- Tokyo's best-preserved old neighborhood — survived WWII bombing and modernization
- Yanaka Ginza shopping street: try menchi-katsu (fried meat croquette), cat-tail donuts
- Yanaka Cemetery: atmospheric walk, especially with early autumn color
- Gallery-hopping: SCAI The Bathhouse (contemporary art in a 200-year-old bathhouse)
- Nezu Shrine: stunning vermillion torii tunnel (mini Fushimi Inari feel)
- Kayaba Coffee: historic kissaten (1938), serves thick toast and tamago sandwiches
- Allow half day for a leisurely wander
- Station: Nippori (JR Yamanote Line) or Sendagi (Chiyoda Line)

**Golden Gai (Shinjuku)**
- 6 narrow alleys with 200+ tiny bars, most seating 6-10 people
- Cover charge: typically 500-1,500 JPY per bar (always ask before sitting)
- Solo-friendly bars: Albatross (3 floors, famous staircase), Champion (tourist-friendly), Death Match in Hell (horror theme)
- Bar-hopping strategy: 1-2 drinks per bar, visit 3-4 bars in an evening
- Best time: 8 PM-midnight; many bars closed Sun/Mon
- Some bars have "no tourists" signs — respect this and move on
- Eat dinner first (Omoide Yokocho is across the tracks for yakitori)
- Station: Shinjuku (any line), 5-minute walk

### Markets & Food

**Tsukiji Outer Market**
- The inner wholesale market moved to Toyosu, but the outer market thrives with 400+ stalls
- Arrive 7-8 AM for freshest selection and shortest queues
- Must-try: tamago-yaki (sweet egg, ~200 JPY), fresh uni on rice (~1,500 JPY), tuna skewers, hojicha from Maruyama Nori
- Turret Coffee: cult-following pour-over in a repurposed turret truck (expect 20 min queue)
- Allow 2-3 hours for grazing breakfast
- Station: Tsukiji (Hibiya Line) or Tsukijishijo (Oedo Line)

**Depachika (Department Store Basements)**
- Isetan Shinjuku: best overall, incredible wagashi (Japanese sweets) floor
- Daimaru Tokyo Station: grab bento boxes for shinkansen rides
- Mitsukoshi Ginza: luxury sweets, free samples if you're polite
- Go around 6-7 PM for discount stickers on bento and prepared foods (up to 50% off)

### Hands-On Experiences

**Cooking Class**
- Recommended: Tokyo Kitchen (Tsukiji area) — small group, market tour + cooking
- Typical menu: dashi from scratch, seasonal sushi rolls, miso soup, tempura
- ~8,000-12,000 JPY for half-day class including market visit
- Book 1-2 weeks ahead; morning classes pair well with Tsukiji visit
- Alternative: Buddha Bellies (Asakusa) for ramen-making class (~7,000 JPY)

**Sumo**
- October is between tournaments (next: November in Fukuoka), so no live basho in Tokyo
- Instead: watch morning practice (keiko) at a sumo stable
  - Arashio Stable: free viewing through a window, no reservation needed, arrive 7:30 AM
  - Other stables require Japanese-language contact or a tour operator (~3,000-5,000 JPY)
- Ryogoku area: Sumo Museum (free, inside Kokugikan), chanko-nabe restaurants
- Recommended chanko: Chanko Tomoegata or Chanko Kawasaki (lunch ~1,500 JPY)

### Gardens & Parks

**Rikugien Garden**
- Edo-period strolling garden, one of Tokyo's finest
- October: early autumn color beginning, especially the central pond area
- Illumination events sometimes run in late October/November — check schedule
- Entry: 300 JPY; allow 1-1.5 hours
- Combine with Yanaka (15 min by train)
- Station: Komagome (JR Yamanote Line, Namboku Line)

**Shinjuku Gyoen**
- 144 acres combining Japanese, French, and English garden styles
- Chrysanthemum exhibition often runs in October/November
- Entry: 500 JPY; no alcohol allowed
- Perfect for a midday rest between activities
- Station: Shinjuku-gyoenmae (Marunouchi Line)

## Kyoto (3-4 Days)

### Temples & Shrines

**Fushimi Inari Taisha (Sunrise Visit)**
- 10,000+ vermillion torii gates winding up Mt. Inari
- CRITICAL: arrive at sunrise (around 6 AM in October) — the lower gates are empty, light filters through perfectly
- Full hike to summit: 2-3 hours round trip, 233m elevation
- The first 30 minutes of trail are the iconic tunnel shots
- Yotsutsuji intersection (halfway): panoramic Kyoto views, good turnaround point if short on time
- Free entry, open 24 hours
- Kitsune (fox) ema at the shrine office are unique souvenirs
- Station: Inari (JR Nara Line, 5 min from Kyoto Station)

**Kinkaku-ji (Golden Pavilion)**
- The gold-leaf covered pavilion reflected in the mirror pond
- Arrive at 9 AM opening; gets extremely crowded by 11 AM
- Entry: 500 JPY (receive a calligraphy charm as your ticket)
- Allow 45 min-1 hour
- Combine with Ryoan-ji (famous rock garden, 15 min walk) and Ninna-ji

**Kiyomizu-dera**
- Wooden stage jutting out from a hillside with sweeping city views
- October: early momiji (maple) color framing the main hall
- Entry: 400 JPY; evening illuminations in late October/November (check dates)
- The Otowa Waterfall at the base: drink from one of three streams (love, school, longevity)
- Walk the Higashiyama district streets below (Sannen-zaka, Ninen-zaka)
- Allow 2-3 hours including the walking streets

**Daitoku-ji Temple Complex**
- Zen temple compound with multiple sub-temples, far fewer tourists
- Highlights: Daisen-in (famous dry landscape garden), Koto-in (moss and maple path), Zuiho-in (Christian-influenced rock garden)
- Sub-temple entry: 400-600 JPY each; visit 2-3
- Excellent for zen meditation experience (see below)
- Allow half day

### Cultural Experiences

**Tea Ceremony (Chado)**
- Recommended: Camellia Garden (near Kenninji) — intimate, English-speaking host, traditional tatami room
- ~3,000-5,000 JPY for a 60-90 min ceremony with wagashi sweet
- Learn: proper entry, how to turn the bowl, appreciating the seasonal scroll and flowers
- Alternative: En (Gion area) for a more casual experience (~2,500 JPY)
- Book 3-5 days ahead

**Kimono Rental & Wearing**
- Recommended: Yumeyakata (multiple locations) — high-quality kimono selection
- Full rental: ~5,000-8,000 JPY for the day (includes dressing, accessories, hair styling)
- Pick up morning (9-10 AM), return by 5-6 PM
- Best areas to wear: Higashiyama, Arashiyama, Gion
- October weather is perfect for comfortable kimono wearing
- Photos at bamboo grove, temple gates, and stone-paved streets are spectacular
- Men's kimono available too — less common but very striking

**Zen Meditation (Zazen)**
- Shunko-in Temple (Myoshinji complex): English-language zazen sessions
  - ~2,000 JPY, 60-90 min including garden tour and tea
  - Reverend Takafumi Kawakami leads sessions; book via website
- Alternative: Taizo-in (also Myoshinji) — quieter, more traditional setting
- Wear comfortable, dark-colored clothing; no shorts

**Pottery / Ceramics**
- Kyoto is the home of Kiyomizu-yaki pottery (400+ year tradition)
- Recommended: Zuikoudo (Higashiyama) — hand-painting workshop on pre-formed pieces
  - ~3,500 JPY, 1-2 hours; shipped to your address after firing
- For wheel-throwing: Asahi Pottery (Gojo area), ~5,000-7,000 JPY
- Visit Kiyomizu-yaki shops on Gojo-zaka (pottery slope) for buying

### Neighborhoods & Markets

**Gion District**
- Kyoto's most famous geisha (geiko) district
- Best geiko/maiko spotting: Hanamikoji-dori around 5:30-6:30 PM (heading to evening engagements)
- Photography etiquette: do NOT block, chase, or touch geiko/maiko — Gion has posted rules
- Gion Corner: 1-hour show featuring tea ceremony, ikebana, gagaku, and maiko dance (~3,150 JPY)
- Yasaka Shrine at the district's edge: free entry, atmospheric at dusk

**Nishiki Market ("Kyoto's Kitchen")**
- 400m covered shopping street with 100+ stalls and shops
- Must-try: yuba (tofu skin), pickled vegetables, matcha anything, dashi-maki tamago, grilled mochi
- Aritsugu: legendary knife shop (since 1560), will engrave your name
- Best time: 10 AM-12 PM (stalls close by 5-6 PM, some earlier)
- Station: Shijo (Karasuma Line) or Kawaramachi (Hankyu)

**Arashiyama**
- Bamboo Grove: arrive before 8 AM — by 10 AM it's shoulder-to-shoulder
- Togetsukyo Bridge: iconic bridge with mountain backdrop
- Monkey Park Iwatayama: 15 min uphill hike, feed wild macaques with Kyoto city views (~550 JPY)
- Tenryu-ji Temple: stunning garden with borrowed scenery of Arashiyama mountains (500 JPY garden only)
- Sagano Scenic Railway (Torokko): 25 min riverside ride, book ahead in October for autumn color (~880 JPY)
- Lunch: try Arashiyama yoshimura for soba with river views

### Sake & Drinks

**Fushimi Sake District**
- Kyoto's historic sake brewing area (Gekkeikan, Kizakura, and 20+ breweries)
- Gekkeikan Okura Sake Museum: history + tasting (~400 JPY with 3 tastes)
- Kizakura Kappa Country: free museum, restaurant with sake flights and craft beer
- Torisei: sake brewery restaurant, pour your own from vending-style machines (~100-300 JPY per glass)
- Best strategy: combine with morning Fushimi Inari visit (10 min walk between them)

**Uji (Day Trip)**
- 20 min by train from Kyoto — birthplace of Japanese matcha culture
- Byodo-in Temple: the building on the 10-yen coin, stunning with autumn color (600 JPY)
- Nakamura Tokichi: legendary tea house (since 1854), matcha soba and parfait (expect 30+ min queue)
- Tsuen Tea: the oldest tea shop in the world (since 1160)
- Omotesando street: matcha ice cream, matcha beer, matcha everything
- Half-day trip; combine with afternoon Philosopher's Path

## Osaka (2-3 Days)

### Must-See Attractions

**Dotonbori**
- Osaka's neon-lit food paradise along the Dotonbori canal
- The Glico Running Man sign: iconic photo spot on Ebisu Bridge
- Must-eat street food:
  - Takoyaki: Wanaka or Creo-Ru (8 pieces ~600-800 JPY)
  - Okonomiyaki: Mizuno (Michelin Bib Gourmand, expect 30 min queue) or Fukutaro
  - Kushikatsu: Daruma (follow the angry chef sign; rule: no double-dipping in communal sauce)
  - Gyoza: Osaka Ohsho for cheap and excellent (~300 JPY for 6)
- Best time: 6-10 PM when the neon is blazing and energy peaks
- Station: Namba (multiple lines)

**Osaka Castle**
- Iconic 16th-century castle (concrete reconstruction, but striking exterior)
- The surrounding park is excellent for early autumn color
- Castle tower: 600 JPY, observation deck with city views
- Museum inside covers Toyotomi Hideyoshi and Osaka's history
- Allow 1.5-2 hours including park walking
- Station: Osakajokoen (JR Loop) or Tanimachi 4-chome (Tanimachi/Chuo Lines)

**Kuromon Ichiba Market ("Osaka's Kitchen")**
- 170+ stalls in a covered market
- Famous for: giant grilled scallops (~500 JPY), sea urchin, eel on rice, wagyu skewers (~1,000 JPY)
- Puffer fish (fugu) tasting available at several stalls (~1,000-2,000 JPY for a portion)
- Best time: 9-11 AM; many stalls close by 4 PM
- Station: Nippombashi (Sakaisuji/Sennichimae Lines)

**Shinsekai**
- Retro neighborhood built in 1912, modeled after NYC (north) and Paris (south)
- Tsutenkaku Tower: kitschy landmark with observation deck (900 JPY)
- Kushikatsu central: Jan Jan Yokocho alley lined with deep-fry shops
- Spa World: massive onsen theme park with themed international baths (~1,500 JPY)
  - Tattoo-friendly on designated floors/times — check current policy
- Billiards, retro arcades, shogi parlors — very local, gritty Osaka atmosphere
- Station: Shin-Imamiya (JR) or Dobutsuen-mae (Midosuji/Sakaisuji Lines)

### Day Trip: Kobe

**Getting There**
- Osaka to Kobe (Sannomiya): 20-30 min by JR, Hankyu, or Hanshin lines (~410 JPY JR)

**Kobe Beef Experience**
- Mouriya: the most famous (lunch sets from ~6,000 JPY for sirloin teppanyaki)
- Ishida: excellent quality, slightly more local feel (~5,000-8,000 JPY lunch)
- Wanto Burger: casual Kobe beef burger (~1,500 JPY) if you want the taste without the splurge
- Lunch service is significantly cheaper than dinner at the same restaurants
- Look for the official Kobe Beef certification mark (chrysanthemum logo)

**Kobe Sights**
- Kitano-cho: European-style "ijinkan" (foreigner mansions) from the 1800s
- Nunobiki Herb Garden: ropeway up the mountain, herb gardens, city/port views (~2,000 JPY round trip)
- Kobe Sake District (Nada): Hakutsuru Sake Brewery Museum (free, tastings included)
- Chinatown (Nankinmachi): smaller than Yokohama's but excellent nikuman (pork buns, ~400 JPY)

## Hakone (1-2 Days)

### Hakone Loop Circuit

The classic sightseeing route — a loop using different transport modes. All covered by the Hakone Free Pass (~6,100 JPY from Shinjuku, 2-day pass; includes round-trip train).

**The Loop (recommended order):**
1. **Hakone-Yumoto to Gora** — Hakone Tozan Railway (switchback mountain train, 40 min)
2. **Gora to Sounzan** — Cable car (10 min)
3. **Sounzan to Togendai** — Hakone Ropeway (30 min, crosses volcanic Owakudani valley)
   - Stop at Owakudani: eat a black egg (kuro-tamago) boiled in sulfuric hot springs — said to add 7 years to your life (~500 JPY for 5 eggs)
   - Mt. Fuji views from the ropeway on clear days (morning has best visibility in October)
4. **Togendai to Hakone-machi** — Pirate ship across Lake Ashi (30 min)
   - The "pirate ship" is kitschy but the lake and mountain views are spectacular
5. **Hakone-machi to Hakone-Yumoto** — Hakone Tozan Bus (35 min)

**Tips:**
- Start early (8-9 AM) to complete the loop comfortably
- October weather can close the ropeway if volcanic gases are high — check morning of
- The loop works in either direction; clockwise (as above) is slightly less crowded

### Hakone Open-Air Museum

- Outdoor sculpture park with works by Picasso, Henry Moore, and more
- Picasso Pavilion: 300+ works in a dedicated building
- Hot spring foot bath inside the museum grounds
- Great in any weather — October's mild temps and potential autumn color make it ideal
- Entry: 1,600 JPY; allow 2-3 hours
- Station: Chokoku-no-Mori (Hakone Tozan Railway)

### Onsen (Hot Springs)

**Tattoo-Friendly Options:**
- Hakone Yuryo: modern facility, private open-air baths available (~1,500 JPY public, ~4,500 JPY private for 1 hour)
- Tenzan Tohji-kyo: outdoor mountain onsen, more rustic feel (~1,300 JPY); ask about tattoo policy (has varied)
- Private onsen in ryokan: many ryokan offer in-room or reservable private baths — easiest solution for tattooed travelers

**Ryokan (Traditional Inn) Experience:**
- A ryokan stay is one of Japan's essential experiences: tatami room, futon, yukata robe, multi-course kaiseki dinner, onsen
- Budget: ~15,000-30,000 JPY/person including dinner and breakfast
- Recommended: Fukuzumiro (since 1890, atmospheric old building), Yama no Chaya (riverside, tattoo-friendly private onsen)
- Book 2-4 weeks ahead for October weekends

### Mt. Fuji Views

- Best viewing: early morning (before clouds build), from the ropeway or Lake Ashi south shore
- October is good visibility month — better than summer humidity
- Hakone-machi/Moto-Hakone: classic "Fuji over torii gate at Lake Ashi" photo spot (Hakone Shrine lakeside torii)
- If Fuji is hidden by clouds, the Hakone Shrine torii gate shot is still excellent

## Hiroshima (1-2 Days)

### Peace Memorial Park & Museum

- Entry: 200 JPY for the museum
- Allow 2-3 hours for museum + park
- Emotionally heavy — the museum's 2019 renovation made it even more impactful with personal artifacts and stories
- A-Bomb Dome (Genbaku Dome): the skeletal ruin preserved exactly as it stood after the blast
- Children's Peace Monument: decorated with thousands of origami cranes
- Cenotaph: frames the Peace Flame and A-Bomb Dome in alignment
- Visit the museum first (opens 8:30 AM), then walk the park grounds

### Miyajima Island (Itsukushima)

**Getting There:**
- Hiroshima to Miyajima-guchi: JR Sanyo Line (25 min) + ferry (10 min, JR ferry free with rail pass)

**Highlights:**
- The "floating" torii gate: partially submerged at high tide, walkable at low tide
  - Check tide tables in advance to plan which experience you prefer
  - Photography tip: high tide with reflections at golden hour is the classic shot
- Itsukushima Shrine: the vermillion shrine built over water (300 JPY)
- Mt. Misen: ropeway up (1,100 JPY one-way, 2,000 JPY round trip), hike down through primeval forest (~1.5 hours descent)
- Friendly deer wander everywhere (unlike Nara, they're slightly less aggressive)
- Must-eat: momiji manju (maple-shaped cakes, ~100 JPY each) — try fresh-fried version
- Grilled oysters: Miyajima is famous for them (~500-1,000 JPY for a plate)
- Omotesando shopping street: conger eel rice (anago-meshi), wooden rice scoops (shamoji)
- Allow at least 4-5 hours; a full day is better if hiking Mt. Misen

## October-Specific Events & Conditions

### Jidai Matsuri (Festival of the Ages) — October 22

- One of Kyoto's three great festivals
- A 2 km procession of 2,000 participants in historical costumes spanning 1,000 years of Kyoto history
- Route: Kyoto Imperial Palace to Heian Shrine (~2 PM arrival at shrine)
- Free to watch from the street; paid seating at Imperial Palace (~2,000 JPY, reserve ahead)
- Best viewing: along Oike-dori or near Heian Shrine for the finale
- Arrives at Heian Shrine around 2:30 PM
- Plan your Kyoto days around Oct 22 to catch this

### Sports Day (October 12, 2026)

- National holiday — expect more domestic tourists at popular spots
- Some businesses may be closed; transit runs on holiday schedule
- Plan for indoor activities or less-popular destinations
- Bonus: festive atmosphere, possible local sports events in parks

### Halloween

- Shibuya Halloween (Oct 31 weekend): massive street gathering, cosplay, controlled chaos
  - Tokyo police have restricted vehicle access in recent years; check 2026 status
  - Costumes range from elaborate anime cosplay to creative absurdity
- Osaka: Dotonbori and Amerikamura get festive
- Theme parks: Universal Studios Japan (Osaka) goes all-out for Halloween Horror Nights
  - Book tickets well in advance; expect massive crowds

### Autumn Foliage Status (Koyo)

October is the **beginning** of koyo season — it starts in the north and mountains, reaching major cities in November.

**What to expect in October:**
- **Hakone**: early color by mid-late October, especially at higher elevations
- **Kyoto**: mostly green in early-mid October; first hints of color by late October around Eikando and Tofuku-ji
- **Tokyo**: green through October; peak not until mid-November
- **Nikko** (optional day trip from Tokyo): peak color mid-October to early November — the best foliage option during this trip window
- **Hiroshima/Miyajima**: similar to Kyoto, late October for earliest color

**Best October foliage spots on this itinerary:**
1. Hakone (ropeway views, Lake Ashi area)
2. Uji (Byodo-in surroundings)
3. Philosopher's Path (early turning trees)
4. Arashiyama (mountain backdrop may show hints)

### Seasonal Food (October Highlights)

- **Sanma (pacific saury)**: grilled whole, peak season — find at izakaya everywhere, ~500-800 JPY
- **Matsutake mushroom**: Japan's prized autumn fungus, in rice (matsutake gohan), soup, or grilled — expensive but worth one splurge (~2,000-5,000 JPY for a dish)
- **Kuri (chestnut)**: in wagashi, mont blanc desserts, kuri gohan (chestnut rice)
- **Shinmai (new rice)**: the year's first harvest, noticeably fluffier and sweeter
- **Kabocha (pumpkin)**: in tempura, soup, and desserts
- **Nashi (Japanese pear)**: crisp and juicy, available at every convenience store
- **Kaki (persimmon)**: both sweet (fuyu) and astringent (hachiya) varieties
- **Sake**: autumn releases (hiyaoroshi) — aged over summer, rich and mellow. Perfect season for sake tasting
- **Seasonal sweets**: chestnut yokan, sweet potato daifuku, momiji manju at peak freshness
