# Plan B and Contingencies — Japan October 2026

> October in Japan averages 10-12 rainy days. Typhoon season runs through mid-October. Always have an indoor backup ready.

---

## Rainy Day Alternatives by City

### Tokyo

#### Museums and Galleries
- **Tokyo National Museum** — Ueno Park. Japan's largest museum with 110,000+ objects. The Honkan (Japanese Gallery) alone needs 2-3 hours. 1,000 JPY.
- **Mori Art Museum** — Roppongi Hills 53F. Contemporary art with city views from the observation deck included in the ticket. 2,000 JPY. Open until 22:00.
- **21_21 Design Sight** — Tokyo Midtown. Tadao Ando-designed museum for design exhibitions. 1,200 JPY. Small but always excellent.
- **Nezu Museum** — Omotesando. Pre-modern Japanese/Asian art in a stunning Kengo Kuma building with a hidden garden. 1,300 JPY.

#### Indoor Experiences
- **TeamLab Borderless** — Azabudai Hills. 2-3 hours of immersive digital art. Pre-book required. Actually better on rainy days (fewer crowds if people cancel).
- **Depachika (department store basement food halls)** — Isetan Shinjuku, Mitsukoshi Ginza, or Takashimaya Nihonbashi. Free to browse; incredible tastings and bento. Budget 2,000-5,000 JPY for a gourmet lunch spread.
- **Manga cafes** — Private booth, unlimited manga, free drinks, showers, WiFi. Chains: Gran Cyber Cafe Bagus, Manboo. 400-600 JPY/hour.
- **Akihabara exploration** — Multi-floor electronics stores, retro game arcades (Super Potato, Hey!), anime shops. Entirely indoors.
- **Sento / Onsen** — Thermae-Yu (Shinjuku, open 24h, 2,600 JPY). A rainy afternoon soak is the perfect recovery activity.

#### Rainy Day Meal Plan
- Extended lunch at Tokyo Ramen Street (under Tokyo Station — 8 top ramen shops in one hallway)
- Afternoon tea at a hotel lounge (Peninsula Tokyo, Aman Tokyo)
- Depachika snack crawl followed by a kissaten coffee stop

### Kyoto

#### Covered and Indoor Options
- **Nishiki Market** — Fully covered 400m shopping arcade. 130+ food stalls and shops. Opens 9:00. Spend 2-3 hours sampling pickles, mochi, grilled seafood, tofu donuts. Free to browse.
- **Manga Museum** — Former elementary school converted to a manga library. 900 JPY. Read from 300,000+ volumes. Half-day easy.
- **Sanjusangendo** — 1,001 life-size golden Kannon statues in a 120m hall. Stunning in any weather. 600 JPY. Near Kyoto Station.

#### Temple Interiors (Best in Rain)
- **Kenninji Temple** — Gion's oldest Zen temple. Twin Dragons ceiling painting, beautiful rock garden viewed from inside. Rain makes the garden moss glow. 600 JPY.
- **Ryoan-ji** — The famous rock garden is viewed from a covered veranda. Rain adds atmosphere. 500 JPY.
- **Nanzen-ji** — The massive Sanmon gate interior is sheltered; Tenju-an sub-temple garden is viewed from inside. 600 JPY combined.

#### Tea Ceremony
- **Camellia Tea Ceremony** — English-language ceremony in a Gion townhouse. Around 3,000 JPY. Book 1 day ahead.
- **En Tea Ceremony** — Near Kinkaku-ji. Small group, matcha plus wagashi. Around 2,500 JPY.
- **Ippodo Tea** — Not a ceremony but a tea tasting room where staff teach you to brew. Free tasting; teas from 1,000 JPY.

#### Kyoto Rainy Day Itinerary
Morning: Nishiki Market snack crawl (9:00-11:00) then Sanjusangendo (11:30-12:30). Lunch at a covered-arcade restaurant. Afternoon: Tea ceremony (14:00-15:00) then Kenninji Temple (15:30-16:30). Evening: Pontocho alley restaurants (covered).

### Osaka

#### Indoor Food Halls and Markets
- **Kuromon Market** — Covered market. Street food breakfast/lunch. See the Hidden Gems guide for early-morning strategy.
- **Dotonbori indoor areas** — Much of the restaurant strip has covered arcades. Shinsaibashi-suji is a fully covered 600m shopping street.
- **Umeda Food Halls** — Basement floors of Grand Front Osaka and Lucua. Hundreds of restaurants.

#### Osaka Aquarium Kaiyukan
- One of the world's largest aquariums. Whale sharks, manta rays, Pacific Rim ecosystems. 2,700 JPY.
- Location: Tempozan, Osakako Station
- Allow 2-3 hours. Less crowded on rainy weekdays (locals assume it will be packed and stay home).

#### Other Indoor Options
- **Osaka Museum of History** — Full-floor recreations of historical Osaka. Views of Osaka Castle from upper floors. 600 JPY.
- **Instant Ramen Museum** — Ikeda (30 min from Umeda). Make your own Cup Noodle. 500 JPY + 500 JPY for custom cup. Book chicken ramen workshop in advance.
- **Spa World** — Shinsekai. Themed international baths across multiple floors. Full day pass around 2,700 JPY. Has saunas, pools, restaurants.
- **Namba Grand Kagetsu** — Yoshimoto comedy theater. Manzai (stand-up comedy duo) performances. Some physical comedy transcends language. Around 4,500 JPY.

## Typhoon Protocol

October typhoons are possible (though late October risk is lower). If a typhoon warning is issued:

1. **Check:** Japan Meteorological Agency (jma.go.jp) and NHK World app
2. **Stay indoors** during the typhoon (usually 12-24 hours)
3. **Trains will stop** — JR and private lines suspend service proactively. Do not go to the station.
4. **Stock up** — Buy water, onigiri, and snacks from a konbini the day before
5. **Post-typhoon** — Trains resume within hours. Next day is often spectacularly clear ("typhoon sunshine")
6. **Hotels:** Your hotel is the safest place. Most are built to withstand typhoons.

## Lost or Stolen Items

- **Lost passport:** Go to US Embassy immediately. Bring a photocopy (keep one in email/cloud) and a passport photo.
- **Lost phone:** Check Find My / Find My Device. Visit the nearest koban (police box) — Japan has an extraordinary 83% lost item return rate.
- **Lost JR Pass:** Cannot be reissued. Buy individual tickets for remaining days.
- **Credit card lost:** Call your bank's international number (save in a separate note/email before the trip).
