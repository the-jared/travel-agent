## Flights: US to Japan

### Route Options

| Route | Airlines | Price Range | Flight Time | Notes |
|-------|----------|-------------|-------------|-------|
| **SFO/LAX to NRT (Narita)** | ANA, JAL, United, AA | $800-1,200 | 11-12h direct | Best deals. Narita is 60-90 min from central Tokyo. |
| **SFO/LAX to HND (Haneda)** | ANA, JAL, Delta | $900-1,400 | 11-12h direct | Haneda is 30-45 min from central Tokyo. Worth the premium. |
| **SEA/PDX to NRT** | ANA, Delta | $850-1,200 | 10-11h direct | Competitive pricing from Pacific NW. |
| **JFK/EWR to NRT/HND** | ANA, JAL, United | $900-1,400 | 14h direct | Fewer deals; book early. |

### Booking Tips

- **Book 3-5 months ahead** — late June to early August for October travel.
- **Google Flights** for price tracking, then book direct with the airline for easier changes.
- **ANA** offers the best economy product on the route (wider seats, good food).
- **Open-jaw** — Fly into Tokyo (NRT/HND), fly out of Osaka (KIX). Saves backtracking and a Shinkansen fare (~$120). Price difference is usually only $50-100 more.
- **Budget option** — Zipair (JAL subsidiary) flies LAX-NRT for $500-700 economy. No frills but gets the job done.

## Japan Rail (JR) Pass Analysis

### The Verdict: 7-Day Pass, Not 14-Day

| Pass Type | Price (2026 est.) | Break-Even Point |
|-----------|-------------------|------------------|
| **7-day Ordinary** | ~$200 (approx. ¥50,000) | 1 round-trip Tokyo-Kyoto ($260 value) |
| **14-day Ordinary** | ~$320 (approx. ¥80,000) | Need 3+ long-distance Shinkansen rides |
| **21-day Ordinary** | ~$410 (approx. ¥100,000) | Rarely worth it |

**Recommendation:** Buy a **7-day pass**, activate it for the travel-heavy middle portion of the trip (e.g., Days 5-11 covering Tokyo to Hakone to Kyoto to Osaka plus day trips). Buy individual tickets for any remaining Shinkansen legs.

### When to Activate

Activate on the day you leave Tokyo for Hakone/Kyoto. This covers:
- Tokyo to Hakone (Odawara Station)
- Hakone to Kyoto
- Kyoto to Nara (day trip)
- Kyoto to Osaka
- Any JR local trains during this window

### How to Buy

- **Online** at [japanrailpass.net](https://japanrailpass.net) — order the exchange voucher before departure, swap it at a JR ticket office in Japan.
- **In Japan** — Buy directly at major JR stations (Narita, Haneda, Tokyo, Kyoto, Shin-Osaka). Bring your passport.

## Local Transit by City

### Tokyo — Suica Card

- **Get a Suica card** at any JR station ticket machine (¥500 deposit + charge amount). Works on all trains, subways, buses, and convenience stores.
- **Alternatively:** Use **Mobile Suica** on iPhone (add via Wallet app — works with international cards).
- **Daily cost:** ¥800-1,500 (~$5-10) depending on how much you move around.
- **Key lines:**
  - **JR Yamanote Line** — The circle line hitting Shinjuku, Shibuya, Harajuku, Tokyo, Akihabara, Ueno. Your lifeline.
  - **Tokyo Metro** — Ginza Line, Hibiya Line, Marunouchi Line cover most tourist spots.
  - **Toei Subway** — Fills gaps. Oedo Line is useful for Tsukijishijo and Roppongi.
- **Last train:** ~midnight. Plan accordingly or budget for a taxi/capsule hotel.

### Kyoto — Bus + Subway

- **Kyoto Bus** is the primary way to reach temples. Flat fare ¥230 per ride.
- **One-day bus pass** was discontinued — just use Suica/ICOCA and tap each ride.
- **Kyoto Subway** — 2 lines (Karasuma and Tozai). Useful for Kyoto Station to Shijo (Kawaramachi area) and Nijo Castle.
- **Rental bike** — Kyoto is flat and bike-friendly. ¥1,000-1,500/day from hotels or cycle shops near Kyoto Station. Best way to explore the Higashiyama district.
- **Tip:** Buses get extremely crowded during autumn leaf season (late October). Walk or bike when possible.

### Osaka — Subway + Osaka Amazing Pass

- **Osaka Metro** — 9 lines, covers everywhere you need. Midosuji Line (red) is the main artery: Shin-Osaka to Umeda to Namba to Tennoji.
- **Osaka Amazing Pass** — **¥2,800 for 1 day** or ¥3,600 for 2 days.
  - Unlimited subway/bus rides
  - Free entry to 40+ attractions including Osaka Castle, Umeda Sky Building, Shinsekai observation deck, Tombori River Cruise
  - Worth it if you do 2+ attractions in a day
- **ICOCA** — Osaka's version of Suica. Interchangeable — your Tokyo Suica works fine here.

### Hakone — Hakone Free Pass

- **Hakone Free Pass** — **¥5,000** (2-day) from Shinjuku via Odakyu Railway.
  - Includes: round-trip Shinjuku to Hakone + unlimited use of Hakone Tozan Railway, cable car, ropeway, pirate ship (Lake Ashi), and buses within Hakone.
  - **Must-do route:** Hakone-Yumoto → cable car up → ropeway over Owakudani (volcanic valley) → pirate ship across Lake Ashi → bus back.
  - Does NOT include Shinkansen — only Odakyu Romance Car or regular Odakyu train.
- **If using JR Pass:** Take Shinkansen to Odawara, then buy a discounted Hakone Free Pass (¥4,000) starting from Odawara instead of Shinjuku.

## Luggage Forwarding — Takkyubin (宅急便)

Japan's luggage forwarding service is a game-changer. Instead of hauling suitcases on trains between cities, ship them ahead.

### How It Works

1. **Drop off** at any convenience store (7-Eleven, Lawson, FamilyMart) or ask your hotel front desk.
2. **Fill out a slip** (hotel staff will help — or use the pre-printed slips from Yamato Transport).
3. **Luggage arrives** at your next hotel by the following day (same-day delivery available for a premium).
4. **Cost:** ¥2,000-2,500 (~$14-17) per standard suitcase, depending on size and distance.

### When to Use

| Leg | Recommendation |
|-----|---------------|
| Tokyo → Hakone | **Ship ahead.** Trains are crowded, ryokan check-in is easier without bags. |
| Hakone → Kyoto | **Ship ahead.** Multiple train transfers otherwise. |
| Kyoto → Osaka | Optional — it's a 15-30 min train ride. But if you have 2+ bags, do it. |
| Hotel → Airport | **Ship ahead.** Enjoy your last day bag-free. Airport pickup at a designated counter. |

### Tips

- Ship by **2-3 PM** for next-day delivery.
- **Airport delivery** (to Narita/Haneda/KIX) requires shipping 2 days before departure. Label clearly with your flight date and time.
- **Yamato Transport (Kuroneko)** is the most popular service — look for the black cat logo.
- Hotels almost always offer this at the front desk. Just ask: "Takkyubin onegaishimasu."
