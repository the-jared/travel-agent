### Flight Booking Tips
- **Target price**: Under $1,000 round-trip economy from US West Coast; under $1,200 from East Coast
- **Best airlines for Japan**: ANA, JAL (superior service, included meals), or United/Delta using miles
- **Open jaw option**: Fly into Tokyo NRT, out of Osaka KIX to avoid backtracking — often same price
- **Credit card points**: Check Chase UR, Amex MR, or Capital One mile transfer partners (ANA is a sweet spot at 75K–90K miles RT)
- **Days to book**: Tuesday/Wednesday departures are typically cheapest
- **Alerts**: Google Flights will email you when prices drop; Hopper will predict optimal buy date

### Accommodation Strategy
Book **free-cancellation** rates now. You can always cancel and rebook if you find better options later. Key platforms:
- [Booking.com](https://www.booking.com/) — Best free-cancellation policies
- [Agoda](https://www.agoda.com/) — Often cheapest for Asia hotels
- [Japanican.com](https://www.japanican.com/en/) — JTB's platform, best ryokan selection
- [Japanese Guest Houses](https://www.japaneseguesthouses.com/) — Curated traditional stays

### Restaurant Reservation Tips
- **High-end sushi/kaiseki**: Many top restaurants only accept reservations through hotel concierges or booking services like [TableAll](https://www.tableall.com/) and [Pocket Concierge](https://pocket-concierge.jp/en/). These charge a booking fee ($10–30) but are worth it.
- **Tabelog**: Japan's #1 restaurant review site. Use [tabelog.com/en](https://tabelog.com/en/) to research, but many listings require Japanese to book. Use your hotel concierge.
- **Michelin Guide Tokyo 2026**: Tokyo has more Michelin stars than any city on Earth. Budget splurge meals strategically.
- **Ramen & casual dining**: No reservation needed. Just show up and queue (lines move fast).
