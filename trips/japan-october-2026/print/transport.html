    Recommendation: Buy a 7-day pass, activate it for the travel-heavy middle portion of the trip (e.g., Days 5-11 covering Tokyo to Hakone to Kyoto to Osaka plus day trips). Buy individual tickets for any remaining Shinkansen legs.

    When to Activate

    Activate on the day you leave Tokyo for Hakone/Kyoto. This covers:

  • Tokyo to Hakone (Odawara Station)
  • Hakone to Kyoto
  • Kyoto to Nara (day trip)
  • Kyoto to Osaka
  • Any JR local trains during this window

    • Hakone — Hakone Free Pass

  • — **¥5,000** (2-day) from Shinjuku via Odakyu Railway.
  • unlimited use of Hakone Tozan Railway, cable car, ropeway, pirate ship (Lake Ashi), and buses within Hakone.

  • Take Shinkansen to Odawara, then buy a discounted Hakone Free Pass (¥4,000) starting from Odawara instead of Shinjuku.

    Luggage Forwarding — Takkyubin (宅急便)

    Japan's luggage forwarding service is a game-changer. Instead of hauling suitcases on trains between cities, ship them ahead.

    How It Works

  • **Drop off** at any convenience store (7-Eleven, Lawson, FamilyMart) or ask your hotel front desk.
  • **Fill out a slip** (hotel staff will help — or use the pre-printed slips from Yamato Transport).
  • **Luggage arrives** at your next hotel by the following day (same-day delivery available for a premium).
  • **Cost:** ¥2,000-2,500 (~$14-17) per standard suitcase, depending on size and distance.

    • When to Use

    LegRecommendation Tokyo → Hakone**Ship ahead.** Trains are crowded, ryokan check-in is easier without bags. Hakone → Kyoto**Ship ahead.** Multiple train transfers otherwise. Kyoto → OsakaOptional — it's a 15-30 min train ride. But if you have 2+ bags, do it. Hotel → Airport**Ship ahead.** Enjoy your last day bag-free. Airport pickup at a designated counter.

    Tips

  • for next-day delivery.
  • (to Narita/Haneda/KIX) requires shipping 2 days before departure. Label clearly with your flight date and time.
  • is the most popular service — look for the black cat logo.
  • Hotels almost always offer this at the front desk. Just ask: "Takkyubin onegaishimasu."
