japan-october-2026 — Checklists

Print and check off with a pen as you go.

Pre-Trip (2-4 Months Before)

Passport — Valid for duration of stay (Japan does not require 6-month validity, but check your airline's policy). Renew if expiring before January 2027.
Visa check — US citizens are visa-free for up to 90 days as a tourist. No application needed. Just show up with a return ticket.
Book flights — Target booking window: June-August 2026 for best October prices. Consider open-jaw (into Tokyo NRT/HND, out of Osaka KIX).
Book accommodations — Especially ryokans (Hakone/Kyoto) and Kyoto hotels (autumn leaf season demand). Cancel-free bookings first, optimize later.
Order JR Pass — Purchase exchange voucher online at japanrailpass.net. 7-day Ordinary pass recommended.
Travel insurance — Purchase coverage for medical, trip cancellation, and lost baggage. World Nomads or SafetyWing.
Register on Visit Japan Web — [vjw.digital.go.jp](https://vjw.digital.go.jp). Pre-register immigration, customs, and quarantine info. Generates QR codes for faster arrival processing.
Research eSIM options — Ubigi, Airalo, or Holafly. Buy before departure, activate on arrival. Unlimited data plans ~$20-30 for 14 days.
Download apps:
- [ ] Google Maps — Download offline maps for Tokyo, Kyoto, Osaka
- [ ] Suica/Mobile Suica — Add to Apple Wallet (iPhone) or Google Pay
- [ ] Google Translate — Download Japanese offline language pack + camera translation
- [ ] Navitime or Japan Travel by Navitime — Best for train schedules and route planning
- [ ] Tabelog — Japan's top restaurant review app (like Yelp but accurate)
- [ ] PayPay — Japan's popular mobile payment (optional but useful)
Book key experiences — TeamLab, any Michelin restaurants, cooking classes, popular ryokans
Notify bank/credit card company — Inform them of Japan travel dates to avoid fraud blocks
Check credit card foreign transaction fees — Use a no-FTF card (Chase Sapphire, Capital One Venture, etc.)

1 Week Before Departure

Confirm all bookings — Hotels, flights, any pre-booked activities. Print or save confirmations offline.
Activate eSIM — Install the eSIM profile (don't activate data yet — do that on landing).
Withdraw cash — Get ~$300 USD worth of Japanese yen from your bank (better rate than airport exchanges). Or plan to withdraw at 7-Eleven ATMs on arrival.
Print key documents:
- [ ] Flight itinerary (outbound + return)
- [ ] JR Pass exchange voucher
- [ ] Hotel confirmations (first and last hotel at minimum)
- [ ] Travel insurance policy number and emergency contact
- [ ] Visit Japan Web QR codes (screenshot works too)
Share itinerary — Send trip details to a trusted contact back home.
Check weather forecast — October in Japan: 15-22C (59-72F). Chance of typhoon early October (rare but possible). Pack layers.
Charge devices — Japan uses Type A plugs (same as US). No adapter needed for American electronics.
Set up Suica in Apple Wallet — Add a Suica card to iPhone Wallet and load ¥2,000-3,000 initial balance.

Packing Checklist — October Layers

Clothing (October: 15-22C / 59-72F, occasional rain)

Light jacket or packable down — Mornings and evenings get cool, especially in Kyoto
Long-sleeve shirts (3-4) — Layers are key
T-shirts (3-4) — For warmer midday hours
Pants/jeans (2-3) — At least one pair that's comfortable for all-day walking
Shorts (1) — Optional, for warmer early-October days
Light sweater or hoodie (1-2) — Temple visits in the morning can be chilly
Rain jacket or compact umbrella — October has moderate rainfall. A packable rain shell beats an umbrella for mobility.
Walking shoes — THE most important item. You will walk 15,000-25,000 steps daily. Broken-in sneakers or trail shoes. NOT new shoes.
Slip-on shoes — For temples, ryokans, and restaurants where you remove shoes constantly. Easy on/off is essential. Vans slip-ons, loafers, or similar.
Socks (5-6 pairs) — You'll be taking shoes on/off at temples. Clean, presentable socks matter. No holes.
Sleepwear (1 set) — Ryokans provide yukata, but hostels/hotels may not.
Underwear (5-6) — Coin laundry available everywhere; pack for ~5 days and wash.

Toiletries & Health

Prescription medications (bring extra + copy of prescription)
Sunscreen — October sun is deceptive
Lip balm
Hand sanitizer
Bandages / blister pads — For the walking. Bring more than you think.
Basic pain reliever (ibuprofen/acetaminophen) — Japanese pharmacies have different formulations
Allergy meds if needed
Earplugs — Essential for capsule hotels and hostels

Tech

Phone + charger (USB-C or Lightning)
Portable battery pack (10,000+ mAh) — Long days, heavy phone use for maps/translation
Earbuds/headphones — For Shinkansen, flights, and unwinding
Camera + charger (optional — phone cameras are great now)
Universal adapter — NOT needed for US travelers (Japan uses Type A, same as US)

Bags

Main bag — 40-55L backpack or small rolling suitcase. Takkyubin-friendly size. Remember: you'll navigate train station stairs.
Day pack — Small backpack or crossbody bag for daily exploration. Must fit: water bottle, rain layer, snacks, portable charger.
Packing cubes — Highly recommended for staying organized across 4+ hotels.
Reusable bag — Japan charges for plastic bags (¥3-5). Bring a foldable tote.

Documents & Money

Passport
Flight tickets (printed or digital)
JR Pass voucher
Hotel confirmations
Travel insurance card/document
Credit card (no foreign transaction fees)
Debit card (backup ATM access)
Japanese yen cash (¥30,000-50,000)
Copy of passport (photo page) — Keep separate from passport

Arrival Day in Japan

Turn on eSIM data — Switch to Japan eSIM profile in Settings.
Immigration — Have your Visit Japan Web QR code ready. Typical wait: 20-60 min.
Customs — Show QR code. Usually waved through for tourists.
Exchange JR Pass voucher — Find the JR ticket office (Midori no Madoguchi) at the airport. Exchange voucher for actual pass. Set activation date for when you'll need it (not necessarily today).
Get Suica card — If not using Mobile Suica, buy a physical card at a JR ticket machine. ¥500 deposit + ¥2,000-3,000 initial charge.
Withdraw cash at 7-Eleven ATM — Inside the airport or at the first 7-Eleven you see. Withdraw ¥30,000-50,000 to start.
Get to your hotel:
- From Narita: N'EX to Tokyo Station (60 min, ¥3,250)
- From Haneda: Keikyu Line to Shinagawa (15 min, ¥300)
Check in and rest — Don't try to do too much on arrival day. Jet lag is real (13-17 hour time difference from US).
Evening: Walk the neighborhood, find a local ramen shop or konbini for dinner. Get oriented.

Return Home Checklist

Ship luggage to airport — Use Takkyubin 2 days before departure. Drop at hotel front desk or nearest konbini.
Return Suica card (if physical) — Get your ¥500 deposit back at any JR ticket counter. Or keep it as a souvenir.
Last-minute souvenirs — Airport duty-free shops at NRT and KIX have excellent Japanese snack selections (often cheaper than city shops).
Tax-free shopping — If you made tax-free purchases, customs may check the sealed bags. Keep receipts stapled in your passport.
Eat one last meal — Airport ramen at Narita is genuinely good. Don't rush past it.
Check in online — Most airlines allow check-in 24 hours before departure.
Arrive at airport 3 hours early — Japanese airports are efficient, but security and immigration can have lines during peak hours.
Fill out departure card — If required (sometimes provided on the plane or at the gate).
Switch eSIM back — Deactivate Japan eSIM, reactivate your home carrier.
Adjust watch/phone — Time zones will auto-update, but mentally prepare for the date-line weirdness (you may "gain" a day flying east).
